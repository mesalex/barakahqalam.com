How to Create Beautiful Coloring Pages at Home

A step-by-step tutorial on designing your own Islamic coloring pages using free software and simple design principles.

July 5, 2026

You don't need expensive software or professional art training to create beautiful Islamic coloring pages. With free tools and a few basic principles, you can design pages that are both spiritually meaningful and artistically satisfying.

Tools You Need

All of these are free:

  • Inkscape: (vector graphics) — best for geometric patterns
  • GIMP: (raster graphics) — good for hand-drawn styles
  • Canva: (web-based) — easiest to start with
  • Paper and pencil: — the most accessible of all!

    • Method 1: Tracing Existing Patterns

    Start by finding public-domain Islamic patterns (many available from museum collections). Print them lightly, trace the outlines with a fine pen, and photocopy or scan.

    Method 2: Creating Geometric Patterns

  • Start with a circle
  • Divide into equal segments (6, 8, 12...)
  • Draw connecting lines between points
  • Erase the construction lines
  • Thicken the remaining lines for a clean coloring page

    • Pro tip: Inkscape has a "tiled clones" feature that automatically creates tessellations!

    Method 3: Hand-Drawn Arabesques

    Arabesques are flowing, plant-inspired patterns. They're more freeform than geometric patterns. Start with:

  • A central stem or spiral
  • Branching curves
  • Leaves and flowers at the ends
  • Repeat symmetrically

    • Design Tips

  • Line thickness: Use 1.5–2pt lines for good visibility when printed
  • Spacing: Leave enough room for colored pencils (avoid overly tight patterns)
  • Test print: Always print a test page to check line clarity
  • Single-sided: If making a book, keep designs on right-hand pages

    • Turn Your Designs Into a Book

    Once you've created 20+ pages, consider compiling them into a coloring book! Our upcoming guide covers the full self-publishing process on Amazon KDP.
