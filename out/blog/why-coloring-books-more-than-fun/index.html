Why Coloring Books Are More Than Just Fun

Explore the therapeutic benefits of coloring and how it can help reduce stress, improve focus, and spark creativity in both adults and children.

July 14, 2026

Adult coloring books have surged in popularity over the past decade — and for good reason. Far from being a mere childhood pastime, coloring offers profound psychological, emotional, and even spiritual benefits.

The Science of Coloring

Research in art therapy has shown that coloring:

  • Reduces anxiety: The repetitive motion lowers amygdala activity
  • Improves focus: Coloring activates the frontal lobe, enhancing concentration
  • Promotes mindfulness: Like meditation, coloring keeps you in the present moment
  • Sparks creativity: Even structured coloring stimulates creative neural pathways

    • Why Islamic Coloring?

    Coloring Islamic art adds another dimension. As you fill in intricate arabesques, geometric patterns, or Arabic calligraphy, you're also:

  • Engaging with centuries of artistic tradition
  • Reflecting on spiritual concepts (the infinite, unity, divine beauty)
  • Learning about Islamic visual culture in a hands-on way
  • Creating artwork that carries personal meaning

    • Coloring as Spiritual Practice

    Many Muslims find that coloring the 99 Names of Allah or Quranic verses deepens their connection to the text. The slow, deliberate act of coloring creates space for contemplation. Each stroke can be a form of dhikr (remembrance).

    Tips for Your Coloring Practice

  • **Start your session with intention** — even a brief dua or reflection
  • **Choose colors mindfully** — consider the emotional or spiritual quality of each hue
  • **Don't rush** — the process matters more than the finished page
  • **Reflect on the design** — what does the pattern teach you?
  • **Share your work** — inspiring others multiplies the benefit
